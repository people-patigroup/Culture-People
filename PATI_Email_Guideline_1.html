📧 Email Guideline

PATI Group - Internal Communication Standard
✨ Email = Decision Tool | All important decisions MUST go through Email
📱 When to Use: Email vs Chat vs Call ▼
Channel Use When Do NOT Use When
💬Chat (Lark) • Quick questions
• FYI updates
• Team coordination
• Casual conversation		 • Need approval
• Formal decisions
• Cross-functional requests
📧Email • Approval needed
• Consult feedback
• Official communication
• Decisions with impact		 • Instant messaging
• Casual updates
📞Call (Phone/Meet) • URGENT crisis
• Complex discussion
• Sensitive topics		 • Can be written
• Needs documentation
✍️ Email Subject Format (Mandatory) ▼
[TYPE - TEAM/NAME] Brief description

Examples:

  • [APPROVE - MKT/Sơn] Budget increase $5K for Q3 campaign
  • [CONSULT - Tech/Phong] Tech stack decision: React vs Vue
  • [INFORM - Email/Trang] New onboarding process launched
  • [URGENT - Tech/Phong] Server down - customer impact
📋 4 Email Types ▼
Type When to Use Expected Response
APPROVE Need Yes/No decision Clear approval or rejection with reason
CONSULT Need feedback/input for decision Perspectives, suggestions, concerns
INFORM FYI - no action needed Read & acknowledge (optional)
URGENT Crisis/blocker - immediate action Response within 30 min
🎯 The 1-3-1 Rule (Email Body Structure) ▼
1
CONTEXT What's happening
(1-2 sentences)
3
OPTIONS 3 possible paths
(with pros/cons)
1
RECOMMENDATION Your suggested choice
(with rationale)
💡 Why? Forces sharp thinking, gives decision-maker options, speeds up approval
👥 TO/CC Rules (RACI Model) ▼
R

→ TO: 1 person
A

→ CC
C

→ CC
I

⚠️ Critical Rule: TO = 1 person ONLY | CC = Everyone else who needs context

📌 Reply Protocol

  • Always acknowledge receipt: "Received and committed to complete by [date X]"
  • If deadline needs adjustment: "Propose moving deadline to [date X] due to [specific reason]"
  • Never work in silence - Silent execution = communication failure
